Solving Food Access

Meal sites for seniors in Massachusetts

Massachusetts does have a delivery program for the elderly — the home-delivered meals program staff meets elderly citizens at home for a home-delivered meal assessment through the Senior Nutrition Program.

It’s unclear how that overlaps with SNAP programs today. Nationally, only 42 percent of eligible elderly individuals participate in SNAP, compared to 83 percent for all people who are eligible. Old people aren’t taking advantage of food services available to them.

The State’s home delivered meals provide nutritious food to seniors who are unable to leave their homes due to illness, disability or frailty— but how can technology expand it’s reach?

Changing nature of food delivery

In 2015, the USDA approved a pilot to allow senior citizens to order food online through approve government partners for direct home delivery.

The Agriculture Secretary at the time, Tom Vilsack, said "Home delivery of groceries is an important step forward in serving the needs of these vulnerable populations. Allowing homebound seniors and people with disabilities to use their SNAP benefits through government and non-profit home delivery services will help ensure they have access to healthy foods," Secretary Vilsack said, noting that one in five SNAP participants is either elderly or disabled.

In 2017, food delivery partners were selected. The pilot is anticipated to begin in 2019, but does not include Massachusetts.

 

Autonomous vehicles are already here

Mayor Marty Walsh and Massachusetts Gov. Charlie Baker signed executive orders in October 2016 that paved the way for the pilot program for self-driving car testing in Boston.

Optimus Ride is currently testing only in the Raymond L. Flynn Marine Park and a few other parts of the Seaport District controlled by Massport, as well as in Weymouth’s Union Point.


NuTonomy has a little more free reign. The company, which had been testing in the South Boston waterfront, was given citywide testing approval in June 2018 but must notify city officials before mapping or testing in a new neighborhood. In July, nuTonomy expanded mapping activities in the Seaport and South Boston.

Increase civic impact for new technology

As driverless cars take hold of Boston, it’s critical that we think of ways that they can benefit all citizens, not only the wealthy, young or able.

These innovations aren’t just a increase in convenience. For some citizens, they could serve as a transformative way to access fresh, healthy food on demand, more often and more reliably than any other transportation medium.

Future, Today

Kroger and Nuro have partnered to use redesigned autonomous delivery vehicles for grocery delivery as early as next year.

Amazon even has a patent application published with the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office is for an “autonomous ground vehicle” that carries items from a delivery truck to a person’s front door.
