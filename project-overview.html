Feeding Boston

Using data from the U.S. Department of Agriculture’s Food Access Research Atlas, we mapped food insecure seniors over the age of 65 in Massachusetts, with a focus on Suffolk County.

 

Project Overview

Problem Definition

We read a New York Times article on food deserts, that cited two major studies finding that, contrary to general wisdom, poorer neighborhoods have more access to grocery stores, supermarkets and full-service restaurants than affluent neighborhoods. We ran a bunch of regressions in Jupyter Notebooks on the US Department of Agriculture Food Access Research Atlas, and confirmed that in Massachusetts, there is no link between poverty and food deserts.

Through research what we did discover, is that, according to the census there are around 600,000 Massachusetts senior citizens who live more than half a mile from a grocery store, and around 300,000 senior citizens live more than one mile from a grocery store. These figures are similar to those estimated by the Massachusetts Public Health Association.


We then conducted research on the especially severe impact that food deserts have on senior citizens. Senior citizens are more likely to struggle to access food in food deserts because of their overall reduced mobility and inability to drive. Food insecurity caused by food deserts causes stress and other physiological effects on older populations. Food insecurity has been associated with depression, asthma and higher blood pressure. 35.7% of the elderly are foreign-born, compared to 27.6% of Boston’s population, which means that there may be an additional language barrier.

Mapping need

We decided that in order to address the issue of senior food deserts we needed to be able to visualize where food deserts are. As a test case, we focussed on Suffolk County which is the greater boston area, and integrated census maps with Department of Agriculture Data on senior food deserts. We cleaned data using python in Jupyter Notebooks, and used Tableau to create a map that heat-maps need among elderly populations across Suffolk County. This was We discovered that, for example, West Roxbury and Chelsea are home to over a thousand older citizens living in food deserts.
